## Product Context

Product: eduMe

eduMe helps organisations ensure frontline teams:

- know what to do
- follow operational processes
- complete required training
- meet safety and compliance requirements
- can prove that training and operational processes occurred

The system focuses on frontline workers in industries such as:

- retail
- hospitality
- logistics
- manufacturing

These environments typically involve:

- distributed workforces
- high turnover
- operational procedures
- compliance requirements
- limited time for traditional training

## Product Mission

Helping organisations ensure frontline teams are operationally enabled, know what to do, and can prove it.

## Core Product Themes

eduMe operates at the intersection of:

### Operational Knowledge
Distributing SOPs, training and operational updates to frontline workers.

### Workforce Distribution
Ensuring knowledge reliably reaches the correct workers through available channels.

### Compliance Evidence
Providing defensible proof that training, audits and operational processes occurred.

### Operational Readiness
Helping organisations understand whether their frontline teams are prepared to execute operational processes.

## Strategic Direction

eduMe is evolving from a training platform toward an operational enablement system.

Future direction includes:

- operational rollout orchestration
- compliance and operational readiness monitoring
- AI-driven training creation
- automated knowledge distribution
- automated compliance reporting

## AI-Native Product Thinking

If eduMe were designed today it would be built with AI at the core.

AI should help automate:

- identification of operational knowledge gaps
- generation of training from SOPs or policies
- distribution of knowledge to the right workers
- reminders and engagement
- compliance reporting
- operational readiness insights

AI should reduce admin workload and increase operational visibility.

## Product Organisation Philosophy

The product organisation should optimise for learning speed.

Key principles:

### Fast experimentation
Teams should rapidly test ideas and prototype workflows.

### Deep customer research
Product teams must understand frontline environments and operational workflows.

### Timely insight
Research and learning must occur early enough to influence product decisions.

## Key Strategic Risks

- **Market framing risk** — organisations default to traditional LMS systems and fail to recognise the need for frontline operational enablement tools.
- **Compliance uncertainty** — unclear which types of compliance evidence customers most urgently need.
- **Product evolution risk** — legacy architecture may slow transition toward AI-native workflows.

## Key Competitors

Primary competitors:
- Opus
- Axonify

Competitive differences:
- Axonify focuses on learning reinforcement and training engagement.
- Opus focuses on operational SOP management and task execution.

eduMe's advantage lies in:
- engaging content formats
- strong integrations
- distribution of operational knowledge
- ability to reach frontline workers directly.

## Strategic Questions

Important open questions include:

- What compliance evidence do organisations actually need during audits?
- What factors make operational rollouts succeed or fail?
- Which communication channels best reach frontline workers?
- How can AI most effectively automate admin workflows?

These questions guide experimentation and research.
